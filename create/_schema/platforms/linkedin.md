# Platform: LinkedIn

LinkedIn platform spec + writing constraints. Layered on top of [[voice]], does not duplicate voice rules.

When `/linkedin-article` is built, it must Read this file before execution.

---

## Platform DNA

- **Character limits**: no hard cap, but sweet spot is 1,300-2,000 chars for regular posts. Long-form articles up to 3,000
- **Tempo**: professional but human. Personal narratives win — "I" stories with a business lesson attached are the LinkedIn default
- **Algorithm**: dwell time + evaluative engagement (comments > shares > likes). LinkedIn rewards "read + comment", not instant likes
- **Critical truncation**: LinkedIn collapses content at ~210 chars with a "see more" button. If the hook isn't in those first 210 chars, expand rate collapses
- **Ody's audience here is [[builders]] + [[casual]] mixed**: decision-makers, professionals with budgets, industry practitioners. **LinkedIn is Ody's highest commercial-leverage platform** — people like Lamont are active here, barely on X

---

## Content Rules

- **Read [[voice]] first**: hard rules apply on LinkedIn (emoji looks even more unprofessional here than on X, no exclamation marks, no "不是 X 是 Y", no guru voice). All voice.md constraints carry over
- **First line decides everything**: the first ~210 chars must hook. Three patterns that work:
  - "I was wrong about X" — counter-intuitive personal admission
  - "3 months ago I [specific scenario]" — concrete past anchor
  - "Everyone told me to X. I did Y instead." — contrarian positioning
- **Short paragraphs, one idea each**. White space is your friend
- **No hashtags in the body**. If you must use them, max 3, at the very end
- **Links in the first comment, not in the post body** — LinkedIn penalizes external links in the post itself
- **Don't go all-lowercase** — LinkedIn readers expect professional capitalization

## Formats That Work

Ranked by fit with Ody's content types + LinkedIn algorithm preference:

1. **Personal Narrative** (LinkedIn's bread and butter) — "I [did / learned / failed at something specific]" → lesson → actionable takeaway. Ody's battle-report prototype maps directly: Cliff consulting, SodaBOS, Bridgelines pilot are all natural fits
2. **Listicle Post** — "N things I learned about [topic]:" + numbered list, 1-2 sentences per item. LinkedIn readers are more patient than X readers, so each item can be a bit longer
3. **Contrarian Take** — bold statement opening, full reasoning underneath. Requires more support than the X equivalent. A bare hot take won't carry it
4. **Case Study** — "How we went from [before] to [after] in [timeframe]" + real numbers + real process. Ody's AI-product-landing analysis prototype can be reframed as a case study
5. **Document / Carousel Posts** — PDF uploaded as a document, 10-15 slides, one idea + bold headline per slide. **The LinkedIn algorithm boosts document posts** — typical reach is 3-5x a regular post. Ody's xhs newspaper-style pages can be redesigned as LinkedIn carousels (different visual language though)

## Posting Strategy

Industry defaults — Ody's actual cadence to be calibrated after a few months of real posting:

- **Frequency**: 3-5 posts/week. LinkedIn doesn't reward high frequency the way X does. Sweet spot is lower
- **Best times** (target audience timezone): 7-8am, 12pm, 5-6pm on weekdays. Weekend activity is much lower than X
- **Pre-publish engagement hack**: before posting your own piece, leave substantive comments on 10 relevant posts in your timeline. The algorithm primes you that way
- **Don't use LinkedIn scheduler templates** — the platform heavily downweights templated content

## Audience on LinkedIn

- **Mix of [[builders]] + [[casual]]**: professionals, decision-makers, industry practitioners
- **What they want**:
  - Professional development (how to become a better operator)
  - Business insights (not universal truths — specific-context judgments)
  - Real stories with lessons (personal narrative → business conclusion)
  - Tools and methodologies that make them better at their work
- **What they don't want**:
  - Overly casual language (meme tweets, emoji-heavy content)
  - Hot takes without reasoning behind them
  - "The truth is..." / "Here's why..." guru phrasing
  - Follower-farming CTAs ("Like if you agree")
- **Commercial potential**: LinkedIn converts an order of magnitude better than X — people here have budgets. For Ody's builder-consultant positioning, LinkedIn is the densest pool of potential clients

## Repurposing Notes

In Ody's [[repurpose]] chain, LinkedIn is typically the **second station** (after [[xhs]] long-form or [[x]] short threads).

### Expanding from an X tweet
- Take an X short-take that got real traction as the seed
- Add a personal narrative (one specific experience, decision, or failure of yours)
- Expand to 1,300+ chars
- Add professional framing ("In my experience helping three luxury brand teams with AI...")

### Deriving from articles/ long-form
- Pull the core judgment + 1-2 concrete numbers
- **Fully rewrite the hook** — what worked as "counter-intuitive in 280 chars" on X has to become "personal story → business insight" on LinkedIn
- Chinese builder-circle context ("卡兹克路线", "同频读者", "AI 大厂", "飞书") → rewrite with English LinkedIn analogs (e.g. "the rage-blogger style", "people building in the same space", "frontier AI labs", "Slack")
- **Strip any reference that requires Chinese cultural context to parse**
- End with an open question to drive comments (comments are the highest algorithm signal on LinkedIn)

### Carousel conversion
- Ody's xhs newspaper-style pages (information-dense, broadsheet aesthetic) can be redesigned as LinkedIn carousels — but the visual language has to change (LinkedIn prefers clean modern design, not newspaper)
- 10-15 slides, one idea + bold headline per slide, minimal body text
- Full flow in [[repurpose]] phase D

### Cross-platform rewriting
- Keep judgment sharpness but **increase argumentation density** — LinkedIn readers expect reasoning, not a bare X-style hot take
- Add first-hand professional context (consulting experience, project case studies, judgments you've actually made) — this is LinkedIn's core currency
- **Don't change the position; change the context**
